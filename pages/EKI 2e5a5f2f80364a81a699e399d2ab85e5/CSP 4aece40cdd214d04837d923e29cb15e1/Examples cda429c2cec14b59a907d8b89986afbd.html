Usually involve higher order constraints.

The goal is to find the right digits so that the sum is arithmetically correct. (no leading zeroes allowed).

O + O = R + 10 ⋅ X 1 X 1 + W + W = U + 10 ⋅ X 2 , X 2 + T + T = O + 10 ⋅ X 3 , X 3 = F \begin{array}{l} O+O=R+10 \cdot X_{1} \\ X_{1}+W+W=U+10 \cdot X_{2}, \\ X_{2}+T+T=O+10 \cdot X_{3}, \\ X_{3}=F \end{array} O + O = R + 10 ⋅ X 1 ​ X 1 ​ + W + W = U + 10 ⋅ X 2 ​ , X 2 ​ + T + T = O + 10 ⋅ X 3 ​ , X 3 ​ = F ​ ﻿

The X 1 , X 2 , X 3 X_1, X_2, X_3 X 1 ​ , X 2 ​ , X 3 ​ ﻿ variables stand for the digit that gets carried over into the next column by the addition.

All constraints can be shown in a constraint hypergraph :

  1. top square box: alldiff constraint

    Each edge just connects variables to their constraints or contraints with eachother.
